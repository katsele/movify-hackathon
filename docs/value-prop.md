# Value proposition — Belgian Digital Skills Radar

## 1. Who

**Managing partners and talent leads at Belgian digital consultancies (50–500 people)**

Firms like Movify, Projective Group, TriFinance, and the 100+ mid-size Belgian digital consultancies that sell product management, UX design, AI engineering, and development talent to enterprise clients in finance, telecom, and public sector. They operate across Flanders, Wallonia, and Brussels — navigating three regional labour markets, two languages, and a blended workforce of employees and freelancers. Their revenue is directly tied to having the right skills available when clients need them.

**Segment characteristics:**

- 50–500 consultants
- €5M–50M revenue
- Tri-regional operations (Flanders / Wallonia / Brussels)
- Blended workforce (employees + freelancers)
- 12+ digital disciplines beyond pure IT (product, design, AI, strategy)

---

## 2. Why — the job to be done

**Know what skills the Belgian market wants right now — before bench time eats your margin**

### Functional job

Decide which profiles to recruit, upskill, or source as freelancers — based on what Belgian enterprises are actively buying, not on gut feeling or stale annual reports.

### Emotional job

Feel confident walking into a staffing meeting knowing you have data backing your hiring bets. Stop the anxiety of discovering a client needs "AI strategy" profiles and you have zero on the bench.

### Social job

Be seen by clients and candidates as the consultancy that's ahead of the curve — the one that proactively proposes the right skills before the RFP even lands.

### Pain indicators

- **€22K/month** per idle consultant in lost revenue
- **72%** of Belgian employers report they can't fill positions (ManpowerGroup 2025)
- **3–6 month** hiring lag vs. market skill shifts
- **21,000+** unfilled IT vacancies in Belgium alone

---

## 3. What before — current situation

**Gut feeling, annual PDFs, and LinkedIn scrolling**

Today, talent decisions at Belgian consultancies rely on a patchwork of lagging, fragmented signals:

| Current method | Friction |
|---|---|
| Robert Half / Hays annual salary guides | Published once/year, focus on IT/finance — no product, UX, or AI strategy roles |
| VDAB knelpuntberoepen list | Annual, occupation-level (not skill-level), Flanders only, misses 60–80% of digital roles |
| LinkedIn scrolling by partners | Anecdotal, biased toward who they follow, no historical trend, no tri-regional view |
| CRM pipeline + partner intuition | Useful but not triangulated against market data — you see your pipeline, not the market |
| Agoria "Be the Change" reports | 5-year horizon, macro-level (541K unfilled by 2030) — too abstract for quarterly staffing decisions |

### The result

Hiring decisions are reactive. A consultancy discovers "everyone wants dbt engineers" or "AI strategy is suddenly hot" 2–3 months after the market shifted — by which time competitors have locked up the available talent, and bench consultants with yesterday's skills are burning €22K/month.

### Deeper friction points

- **No tri-regional view exists.** VDAB covers Flanders, Le Forem covers Wallonia, Actiris covers Brussels — but no tool integrates all three with a unified taxonomy. A consultancy operating from Brussels and Antwerp has no single dashboard showing demand across both markets. **<u>(in IT market and digital, these actors are not the most relevant)</u>**
- **Non-IT roles are invisible.** Public employment services classify "UX designer" under generic multimedia codes and have no taxonomy entry for "service designer," "AI strategist," or "product manager." The roles Movify sells are literally untrackable in public data. **<u>(less relevant)</u>**
- **Consulting demand is opaque.** When Belfius or SNCB is about to issue a framework contract for AI consultancy, consultancies learn about it through word-of-mouth or when the tender drops — with no leading indicator. **<u>+++</u>**

## 4. How — the solution

**A real-time Belgian digital skills radar across all three regions and all disciplines**

A dashboard that ingests live vacancy data from **four tiers of signal** and maps everything to a **unified taxonomy of 12+ digital disciplines** that matches how consultancies actually sell. **<u>(too much live vacancy data, we need to be ahead f the curve. Red ocean style, bitch. We nee to gather way more data points to become relevant: news, (CEO changing assignment), geoplopitcal,n international marken e.g. US is ahead by X years, use that as iinspiration, european lega market, etc...)</u>**

### Disciplines covered

Product management, service design, UX research, UX/UI design, AI strategy, AI/ML engineering, data science, web development, mobile development, cloud/DevOps, accessibility, design systems.

### Four-tier data architecture

| Tier | Sources | Cost | Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Public APIs** | VDAB Vacature API, Le Forem Opendatasoft, ESCO taxonomy | Free (VDAB needs partnership approval) | Engineering, data, cloud — real-time Flemish/Walloon pulse |
| **2. ATS endpoints + niche boards** | Greenhouse, Lever, Ashby from 100+ Belgian scale-ups; ICTJob.be; Welcome to the Jungle BE; Creativeskills.be | Free–€30K/yr (ICTJob.be partnership) | Product, design, AI roles — the coverage gap |
| **3. Procurement feeds** | TED API v3 + BOSA e-Procurement (enot.publicprocurement.be) | Free | Consulting demand from public sector — 3–6 month leading indicator |
| **4. Community + calibration** | Meetup (ProductTank, UX Belgium, AI Belgium), ManpowerGroup MEOS, Agoria quarterly reports, Google Trends (geo=BE) | Free–€200/mo | Emerging tech signals, macro validation |

### Key capabilities

| Feature | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Tri-regional heatmap** | See demand by Flanders / Wallonia / Brussels — in NL, FR, and EN postings simultaneously |
| **Skill-level granularity** | Not just "developer" but React vs. Angular, AWS vs. Azure, LangChain vs. dbt — via NLP on posting text + Lightcast Open Skills taxonomy (free API, 33K+ skills) |
| **Consulting demand pulse** | Real-time feed from TED/e-Procurement filtered by CPV codes for IT services (72xxx), management consultancy (79411000), UX/design consultancy (72224000) — a 3–6 month leading indicator no competitor offers |
| **Weekly digest** | Automated briefing: "AI strategy demand up 34% QoQ in Brussels, driven by 4 new public-sector frameworks" |
| **Quarterly deep-dive** | Trend analysis with historical comparison, mapped against Agoria/Statbel ground-truth data |
| **CRM overlay (V2)** | Fuse your pipeline data with market signals — see where your bets align with or diverge from actual demand |

### Why it's better than alternatives

- **Belgian-native:** Built for Belgium's tri-regional, trilingual complexity — not a global tool with Belgium as a rounding error
- **Full-discipline coverage:** Product, design, AI strategy, accessibility — not just IT/engineering
- **Procurement signal:** The only tool that turns public tender data into a consulting demand indicator
- **Mid-market pricing:** Designed for €5M–50M consultancies, not €500M enterprises

## 5. What after — the outcome

**Proactive talent strategy instead of reactive scrambling**

### Operational outcomes

- Hire 1–2 quarters ahead of demand instead of 1–2 quarters behind
- Reduce bench time by matching consultant supply to actual market signals
- Spot emerging skills (dbt, AI agents, accessibility consulting) before competitors
- Win more tenders by pre-positioning the right profiles
- Direct upskilling investments toward skills with rising demand, not gut feeling

### Economic case

If a 100-person consultancy reduces bench time by even 3.5 percentage points (from 20% idle to 16.5%), that's roughly **€300K–400K in recovered annual revenue**. The radar pays for itself if it prevents a single quarter of mis-hiring.

At a top-performing consultancy achieving 80% billable utilization, every additional percentage point of utilization at €1,100/day rates across 100 consultants generates approximately **€220K annually**.

### Strategic case

Your firm becomes the one that walks into client meetings saying *"we see AI strategy demand accelerating in Belgian banking — here's the team we've already assembled"* instead of scrambling to recruit after the brief lands.

### What becomes possible

- **Data-backed staffing meetings** replace "what are you hearing?" anecdotes
- **Proactive client conversations** about upcoming skill needs, based on tender pipeline intelligence
- **Targeted freelancer sourcing** for skills trending up before the wave hits
- **Evidence-based training budgets** directed at skills with demonstrable rising demand

4. **Mid-market pricing** accessible to 50–500 person consultancies

Every incumbent either skips Belgium, skips non-IT roles, or costs €30K+/year.

## Key risks to validate

1. **LinkedIn blind spot.** Senior and consulting roles that only appear on LinkedIn (estimated 60–80% of product/UX/strategy roles) cannot be accessed compliantly without a €6K+ Talent Insights subscription. Mitigation: ATS endpoints + procurement feeds cover much of this, but the gap must be acknowledged transparently.

2. **Willingness to pay.** Consultancies already have access to free (if fragmented) data from VDAB, Agoria, and staffing agencies. The product's value is curation, integration, and the non-IT taxonomy — but this needs validation with 5–10 target customers before building.

3. **NLP on multilingual free-text.** Belgian job postings and tender documents mix NL, FR, and EN — often within a single document. Skill extraction quality is the core technical risk. The data is available, but turning messy text into clean skill tags requires a dedicated labelling pipeline and iterative tuning.

4. **Taxonomy maintenance.** The unified 12-discipline taxonomy that makes this product valuable must be continuously maintained as new skills emerge and role definitions evolve. This is an ongoing operational cost, not a one-time build.
