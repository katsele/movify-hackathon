# Belgium's 2,000+ employee club: Prime targets for digital, UX and AI consulting

Belgium hosts roughly **70–80 organisations that employ more than 2,000 people on Belgian soil**, concentrated in banking and insurance, public transport, postal and telecom operators, federated health and mutualities, food retail and a handful of pharma-chemical giants clustered around Antwerp, Leuven and Wallonia. The vast majority are either incumbents defending market share from digital challengers (Proximus vs. Telenet/Orange; Belfius vs. Revolut/N26) or state-linked operators running on ageing core systems that now need AI, self-service UX and data consolidation — a classic sweet spot for consulting work.

Importantly, this list excludes the many Belgian headquarters of **groups that are globally large but locally small** (Ageas, Solvay corporate, Euronav, Barco, Melexis) as well as ICT vendors that are themselves competitors for digital work (Accenture, Deloitte, Capgemini, Cegeka, Proximus NXT, NRB, Sopra Steria, EY, KPMG, PwC). Employee figures are stated **as "Belgium-only"** where available; where only group-level figures exist, that is flagged.

Belgian financial services are exceptionally concentrated and exceptionally digital-hungry. The **six largest banks — BNP Paribas Fortis, KBC, Belfius, ING Belgium, Crelan and Argenta — posted a collective €9 billion net profit in 2024** and almost all of them are actively scaling AI (KBC's "Kate", Belfius's Jaimy/Ajusto, ING's Scan & Drive, BNPPF's generative-AI pilots).

## Telecom and media: three national champions and two public broadcasters

The sector is tightly oligopolistic after Orange Belgium's takeover of VOO and the Telenet delisting. **Proximus (12,790 employees, December 2025) has announced AI-driven attrition** — replacing departing staff only "partially" while building out its "Proximus Ada" AI/cybersecurity centre. Telenet (~3,300) is mid-transformation as a Liberty Global/Fluvius joint venture branded under **NetCo/Wyre**. **DPG Media (~6,000 Benelux)** and **Mediahuis (~5,000 Benelux)** drive most Dutch-language news plus HLN/VTM, with persistent paywall-UX and AI-personalisation spend. **VRT (~2,100) and RTBF (~2,000)** are the public broadcasters, both running multi-year streaming platform rebuilds (VRT MAX, Auvio). **Rossel** group (Le Soir, Sudinfo, L'Avenir) employs roughly 3,500 across media and printing.

## Retail and e-commerce: Colruyt, Delhaize and the discount surge

Belgian food retail is a **five-horse race** — Colruyt, Ahold Delhaize, Carrefour, Aldi and Lidl — collectively accounting for well over three-quarters of grocery spend. **Colruyt Group is the largest private employer in the country, with around 33,000 staff, the vast majority in Belgium** (the group is Belgium-Luxembourg-France). Its Xtra loyalty app, SmartWithFood, Collect&Go online grocery and the ongoing Match/Smatch conversion all generate continuous UX/analytics workload.

**Delhaize Belgium** (~9,500 after the 2023–24 franchising transition) is heading deeper into its "affiliated entrepreneur" model, which demands a completely new B2B2C digital stack. **Carrefour Belgium** (~8,500, 710 stores) is trialling Reckon.AI-powered autonomous stores ("Buy Bye"). **Aldi Belgium (~8,000)** and **Lidl Belgium (~10,000, 320 stores)** are investing heavily in app-based loyalty and predictive replenishment. **Action Belgium (~3,500)** and **IKEA Belgium (~4,500)** round out the non-food pack; **Decathlon Belgium (~2,500)** and **Brico/Hubo/Gamma (collectively ~4,000)** are further candidates. In pure e-commerce, **bol (Ahold Delhaize, BE+NL ~2,500)** and **Coolblue (~7,500 BE+NL)** both rank as top-tier digital employers but only partially Belgium-based.

## Energy, utilities and water: a transition-driven pipeline

The sector is being reshaped simultaneously by nuclear life extension, smart-meter rollout and the Fit-for-55 package. **Engie/Electrabel employs roughly 5,000 in Belgium** (of ~17,000 in the Engie Belgium perimeter) and is running the twin-reactor Doel 4/Tihange 3 restart, a massive programme management opportunity. **Luminus (EDF) employs ~2,100**, mostly on distributed-generation and trading. 

Grid operators are a distinct cluster: **Elia (~2,800 in Belgium, 4,600 group)**, the Flemish DSO **Fluvius (~5,500)**, the Walloon **ORES (~2,400)**, and the Brussels DSO **Sibelga (~1,100, below threshold)**. **Fluxys (~1,200)** is below 2,000 but strategically important for hydrogen. In water: **De Watergroep (~1,500)**, **Vivaqua (~1,400)**, **SWDE (~1,500)** and **Aquafin (~1,100)** all sit just below the line but are heavy smart-metering spenders. Smart-meter rollout alone — **Fluvius targeting all Flemish prosumers by end-2025** — has generated a multi-year data platform programme.

## Transport, logistics and post: every mobility operator qualifies

Public transport operators cross the threshold comfortably and are all mid-digitalisation. **SNCB/NMBS employs roughly 17,500 and Infrabel another 10,000**; both are rebuilding ticketing and predictive-maintenance stacks (Brupass XL multi-operator tickets launched 2024). **STIB/MIVB has ~10,000 staff**, **De Lijn ~9,000** and **TEC ~5,000** — all three run active mobile-as-a-service and fleet-electrification programmes. **Eurostar/Thalys** sits under threshold in Belgium.

In postal and parcels, **bpost employs about 24,000 in Belgium (of 40,000+ globally)** and is executing the largest transformation in the sector after the Staci acquisition: **the My bpost app hit 2.37 million monthly active users in 2024**, and 3,500 pick-up points demand heavy UX and logistics-AI work. **DHL Express / Global Forwarding Belgium (~4,000)**, **FedEx's Liège hub (~3,500)**, **UPS Belgium (~1,500, below)** and **Kuehne+Nagel Belgium (~2,000)** complete the logistics majors. Belgian-rooted logistics — **H.Essers (~3,500 BE), Katoen Natie (~3,500 BE), Jost Group (~2,000), Sea-Invest (~2,500)** — all qualify. **Port of Antwerp-Bruges (~1,800, borderline)** and **Brussels Airport Company (~900 direct, but ~25,000 at the platform)** are institutional clients with enterprise-architecture needs. **Brussels Airlines (~3,200)** and **TUI fly Belgium (~1,100, below)** round out aviation.

## Healthcare, social security and mutualities: the underappreciated digital market

The five **sickness funds (mutualities) each qualify**: **Christelijke Mutualiteit/Mutualité Chrétienne (~4,500)** is the largest with 4.5 million members, followed by **Solidaris (~3,500)**, **Helan (~3,000, post-merger)**, **Neutrale Ziekenfondsen (~2,000)** and **Liberale Mutualiteiten (~1,500, borderline)**. They are co-investors in Proximus's Doktr teleconsultation app and have regulatory pressure to modernise member self-service. 

Major university hospitals employ well above threshold: **UZ Leuven (~10,000), UZ Gent (~6,500), CHU de Liège (~5,500), UCL Saint-Luc (~5,500), Erasme/ULB Hôpital (~4,500), UZ Brussel (~3,800), UZA Antwerp (~3,400), AZ Sint-Jan Brugge (~3,000), AZ Delta (~3,500) and ZAS Antwerp (~6,000 post-merger)**. All are live on multi-year EHR-replacement decisions (Epic vs. Nexuzhealth vs. Dedalus) and AI-radiology pilots — classic UX-heavy transformation work.

## Pharma, chemicals and industry: a smaller but richer cluster

Belgium is **the world's second-largest vaccine producer**, so pharma delivers a surprisingly big employer footprint. **GSK employs ~9,000 in Belgium across Wavre, Rixensart and Gembloux**, including ~2,000 R&D scientists, and has committed €330 million to Wavre plus a new Gembloux logistics centre. **Johnson & Johnson Innovative Medicine / Janssen Beerse employs ~5,000** (despite 2024 restructuring). **Pfizer Puurs (~4,500)** is in the middle of a €1.2 billion three-year upgrade. **UCB Braine-l'Alleud (~2,800 in Belgium)** opened a biomanufacturing centre in 2024 and started construction on a new €100 million R&D facility in February 2025. **Takeda Lessines (~2,000)** opened an automated warehouse in April 2024.

In chemicals and materials: **BASF Antwerpen (3,755 employees — Belgium's largest chemical site)**, **INEOS Belgium (~2,500 across Antwerp and projects), Covestro Antwerp (~1,200, borderline), Evonik Antwerp (~1,000, below), Solvay Belgium (~2,500), Umicore Belgium (~3,200) and ArcelorMittal Belgium (~5,000, mostly Gent)**. Automotive manufacturing narrows dramatically with **Audi Brussels closing** — leaving **Volvo Car Gent (~6,500)** and **Volvo Group Trucks Gent (~3,200, with ~5,000 Volvo Group total in Belgium)** as the last volume plants. **AB InBev employs over 2,800 in Belgium** across five breweries and its Leuven HQ; **Agristo, Mydibel, Clarebout (potato), Puratos (~2,500 BE of 10,000 global), Lotus Bakeries (~1,500, below), Mondelēz Belgium (~1,400, below)** populate food manufacturing. **Barry Callebaut Wieze (~1,200, below threshold)** is the world's largest chocolate factory but lean on HQ staff.

## Public sector and utilities-adjacent: often overlooked

The Belgian federal and regional governments collectively constitute the country's largest employer base but typically procure through framework contracts. Key units exceeding 2,000 with genuine digital budgets include: **Belgian Armed Forces (~25,000)**, **Federal Police (12,300)**, **FOD Financiën / SPF Finances (~20,000)**, **FOD BOSA (policy & support, ~1,500, below)**, **RSZ/ONSS and RVA/ONEM (~5,000+ each)**, **Smals (the shared IT organisation for social security, ~2,100)**, and universities: **KU Leuven (~12,000 including UZ), UGent (~9,500 including UZ Gent), UCLouvain (~6,500), ULB (~6,000 including Erasme), VUB (~3,500), ULiège (~5,500)**. The **Flemish, Walloon and Brussels regional administrations** each employ well above 5,000, with multi-year e-government roadmaps (MijnBurgerprofiel, MonEspace, IRISbox).

## Where the real consulting demand will concentrate in 2026

Three patterns stand out from the data. **First, the banking-insurance cluster is the deepest buyer**: fifteen institutions above threshold, all actively spending on GenAI, mobile UX and core-system modernisation, with Crelan/AXA Bank and post-bpost-bank/BNPPF integrations providing multi-year work. **Second, the state-adjacent incumbents — bpost, Proximus, SNCB, STIB, De Lijn, Fluvius, VRT, mutualities and federal administrations — represent the largest cumulative headcount** and are under political pressure to deliver citizen-grade digital services, but procure slowly through framework contracts. **Third, food retail and pharma manufacturing are digitalising operations (supply-chain AI, computer vision, predictive quality) rather than front-end UX**, so they are better matched to data-engineering and MLOps consultancies than to pure design studios.

The realistic universe of **"named accounts" for a Belgian digital/UX/AI consultancy is therefore roughly 60 buying centres**, of which perhaps 20–25 account for the majority of external consulting spend: the big four banks, the three largest insurers, Proximus, bpost, Colruyt, Ahold Delhaize, Carrefour, SNCB/Infrabel, STIB, De Lijn, Fluvius, Elia, Engie, UZ Leuven, KU Leuven, GSK, Janssen, UCB, VRT/DPG, Euroclear and SWIFT. That short list effectively defines the addressable enterprise market in Belgium today.
