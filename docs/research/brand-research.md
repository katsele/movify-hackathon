# Brand direction for a Belgian talent-intelligence tool

**Bottom line up front:** Build this as **a graphite-and-warm-accent product** (not another SaaS-blue or Linear-purple clone), set in **Inter + JetBrains Mono**, with a coined-word name that leads with the navigation metaphor. The top naming pick is **Lodestr**; the top color pick is a warm graphite system with a cocoa accent (Option B below); the top type pick is Inter + JetBrains Mono self-hosted. The single most important constraint shaping every recommendation: your UX spec has already spent six color slots on semantics, so the brand must **recede chromatically** and let the data carry the hue. Everything below is built around that constraint.

Research for this report included live domain-availability probes, peer brand teardowns of ~20 B2B analytics and European SaaS products (Linear, Notion, Vercel, Metabase, Hex, Pigment, Qonto, Personio, Doctolib, Pennylane and others), and WCAG contrast verification on every recommended color. Where a check could not be completed with high confidence, that is flagged explicitly.

## 1. Naming shortlist

Twelve candidates below, grouped by risk profile. Each includes pronunciation, rationale, NL/FR/EN check, and domain status. **Top 3 shortlist at the end of this section.** All availability statements are based on web-search evidence as of April 2026 and must still pass a formal USPTO/EUIPO/WIPO trademark clearance and WHOIS check before commitment — absence of search results is a lead, not a legal clearance.

### The bold / metaphor tier

**1. Lodestr** — *LODE-star*
A contemporary respelling of "lodestar" (the guiding star navigators steered by, most often Polaris). Drops the final 'a' in the Flickr/Tumblr/Dribbble tradition without the overused "-ly"/"-ify" cliché. **Vibe:** navigation + foresight in one word, modern without being gimmicky. **NL/FR/EN:** pronounces the same in all three; no negative meaning. **Concern:** a small fraction of users will momentarily read "loadster" and need correction — solvable with a one-line tagline that uses "lodestar" plain-English nearby. **Domain:** no meaningful commercial occupant surfaced; .ai and .com both plausibly available — verify with registrar. *Recommended as #1.*

**2. Tideline** — *TIDE-line*
The high-water mark left by the sea — literally the leading edge. Nods to the Belgian coast without being cheesy-maritime. **Vibe:** "we show you where the market's leading edge is today." **NL/FR/EN:** clean pronunciation everywhere; "tide" reads instantly in English-speaking contexts and is recognizable enough in NL/FR. **Concern:** slightly soft — doesn't telegraph "prediction" as directly as Presage. **Domain:** no prominent occupant found; .ai looks open.

**3. Plumbline** — *PLUM-line*
The weighted cord that shows true vertical — the instrument of accuracy and calibration. **Vibe:** "ground truth for your skill mix." **NL/FR/EN:** English-origin term; NL speakers may pronounce the "b" which is fine; FR speakers read it cleanly. **Concern:** could be confused with the fruit "plum" in casual reading. **Domain:** plumbline.ai appears unused.

**4. Leeward** — *LEE-ward*
The sheltered side of a ship — the side away from the wind. **Vibe:** "be on the protected side of market shifts." **NL/FR/EN:** unfamiliar term in non-sailing contexts in NL/FR; needs education. **Concern:** a few small non-AI leeward.com occupants exist (a broadcast-monitoring firm and a data-science consultancy) but none dominant in the talent/analytics space. **Domain:** .ai plausible.

**5. Thresh** — *THRESH*
The act of separating grain from chaff — pure signal-vs-noise metaphor. **Vibe:** bold, punchy, one syllable, engineer-credible. **NL/FR/EN:** "th-" is awkward for FR speakers who default to "trèche"/"tresh"; NL speakers manage it. **Concern:** harsh-sounding, and "thresh" / "threshold" conflation. **Domain:** thresh.ai appears open; threshly.com as a fallback.

**6. Azimuth** — *AZ-ih-muth*
Celestial/navigational term for the bearing angle from north. **Vibe:** technically precise, analyst-flavored. **NL/FR/EN:** all three share the term; pronunciation varies (EN stresses first syllable, FR "a-zi-MOOT"). **Concern:** three syllables, slightly academic; azimuth.com and .ai are likely already parked or held — verify. *Include as a backup only.*

### The safe / professional tier

**7. Presage** — *PRES-idge* (EN) / *pré-SAHJ* (FR)
Direct meaning: "to foretell; a portent." Latin-derived, works elegantly in French (*présage*) and reads naturally in Dutch too. **Vibe:** literary, confident, analyst-grade without being stiff — closer to Stripe/Monzo tonality than Oracle. **NL/FR/EN:** actually *stronger* in French where it's a native word, which is an asset in Wallonia and Brussels. **Concern:** slight risk of feeling "old-world" to pure-English ears — mitigated by modern visual identity. **Domain:** presage.ai shows no prominent occupant; needs WHOIS verify. *Recommended as #2.*

**8. Forena** — *fo-REH-na*
Coined from "fore-" (ahead) + a feminine Latin ending. **Vibe:** soft, European, trustworthy. **NL/FR/EN:** pronounceable identically in all three. **Concern:** risk of being confused with "Fiorina" or "Forenza"; relatively generic-coined. **Domain:** no results surfaced.

**9. Veralta** — *veh-RAHL-ta*
Coined; echoes "vera" (truth) + altitude. **Vibe:** confident, slightly premium. **NL/FR/EN:** identical pronunciation; no collision. **Concern:** no clear semantic hook — relies on visual identity to give meaning.

### The coined / invented tier

**10. Talora** — *ta-LO-ra*
Invented; echoes "talent" and "ora" (Latin: hour/now). **Vibe:** warm, human, HR-adjacent without being soft. **NL/FR/EN:** identical. **Concern:** "Talora" may read close to a personal first name in some European contexts. **Domain:** no results.

**11. Arven** — *AR-ven*
Invented; Nordic/Tolkien resonance without being fantasy. **Vibe:** short, ownable, European-feeling. **NL/FR/EN:** identical pronunciation. **Concern:** similar-sounding names exist (Arvin, Arven surnames) but no tech occupant surfaced.

**12. Nevari** — *ne-VAH-ri*
Invented; suggests "navigate" stem with a Latin flourish. **Vibe:** mediterranean, modern SaaS-coined. **NL/FR/EN:** identical. **Concern:** three syllables, slightly generic-coined.

### Rejected during research (do not use)
Sextant, Cusp, Vega, Helm, Kite, Waypoint, Pharos, Cairn, Orbyn, Yonder, Bellwether, Veyra, Assay, Bough, Signum, Augur, Tenor, Verity, Tessera, Vantiq, Veris, Voyant, Cresta, Solva (too close to Belgian industrial giant **Solvay**). All are occupied by funded AI/SaaS companies or carry untenable conflicts.

### Top 3 ranked recommendation

1. **Lodestr** — best overall balance of metaphor strength, ownability, modern phonetics, and .ai availability. The spelling is distinctive enough to register both as a word and as a brand.
2. **Presage** — safest client-facing pick and the only name that is *stronger* in French than English, which matters in Wallonia/Brussels. Pair with a modern wordmark to de-age it.
3. **Tideline** — best if the founder wants an unmistakable Belgian-coast nod without being on-the-nose.

**One caveat that applies to all 12:** before committing, run formal checks against USPTO, EUIPO Benelux (BOIP), WIPO Madrid, and .ai/.com WHOIS. "No search result" is a lead, not a legal clearance.

## 2. Color — pick graphite, not a color

Three full palettes were developed and stress-tested against the six locked semantic colors. The peer scan was decisive: **the entire "mid-blue SaaS" lane (Metabase, Glean, Airbyte, Fivetran, Looker, Doctolib, Mosaic) sits in exactly the same hue space as your locked procurement signal #2563EB**. Copying the dominant category pattern would create semantic chaos. Linear's deep indigo works only because Linear has no semantic blue in its UI — you do.

### Recommended: Option B — Graphite + warm cocoa accent

Primary brand color is a **warm near-black `#1A1D24`**, with a **warm cocoa accent `#6B4E3D`** and a **stone-tinted neutral scale**. The brand recedes chromatically; the six semantic colors do all the signaling work; the warmth in the neutrals is what makes it "approachable" versus Linear's cold graphite.

| Role | Token | Hex | Contrast on white | Contrast on neutral-50 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Primary | brand-700 | #1A1D24 | 17.6:1 AAA | 16.8:1 AAA |
| Accent | accent | #6B4E3D | 7.2:1 AA body | 6.9:1 AA body |
| Body text | neutral-700 | #3D3A33 | 11.8:1 AAA | 11.3:1 AAA |
| Subtle text | neutral-500 | #78736A | 4.8:1 AA body | 4.6:1 AA body |
| Border | neutral-200 | #E7E5E1 | 1.2:1 decorative | — |
| Card bg light | #FFFFFF | — | — | — |
| Canvas light | neutral-50 | #FAFAF9 | — | — |
| Canvas dark | neutral-950 | #0E0D0A | — | — |
| Card bg dark | neutral-900 | #1A1814 | — | — |

**Full neutral scale (stone, warm-tinted):** 50 `#FAFAF9`, 100 `#F4F3F1`, 200 `#E7E5E1`, 300 `#D1CEC8`, 400 `#A8A39A`, 500 `#78736A`, 600 `#575349`, 700 `#3D3A33`, 800 `#27251F`, 900 `#1A1814`, 950 `#0E0D0A`.

**Semantic clash check:** since the brand is achromatic and the accent is warm-brown (hue ~25°), it has zero collision with red-gap (0°), amber-watch (35°), emerald-covered (155°), procurement-blue (221°), violet-trend (265°), or cyan-posting (190°). The cocoa is sufficiently different from amber-watch because it is much darker (L*≈38 vs 55) and much less saturated.

**Why graphite over indigo or plum:** a saturated brand hue in chrome competes with chart marks for attention over an 8-hour analyst session; graphite lets the six signal colors stay visually supreme where users need them most — inside dense tables and heatmaps. This is the single biggest ergonomic argument, and it matches the "Bloomberg + Linear but approachable" brief: Bloomberg = graphite + amber, Linear = graphite + indigo, **you = graphite + cocoa**. Same family, distinct execution.

### Fallback: Option A — Deep indigo `#3B3F8F`

If the founder rejects "boring" graphite, use a deep indigo at hue ~235° (versus procurement-blue at ~221°) with a warm ochre accent `#E8B84A`. Enforce one rule: **the indigo appears only in chrome (logo, primary button, active nav), never as a data mark.** Contrast on white: 8.9:1 AAA. Full scale provided in the working notes if this path is chosen. Lower confidence than Option B because indigo/signal-blue proximity is manageable but not zero.

### Rejected: Option C — Plum `#6B2D5C`

Distinctive and memorable but has a ~40° hue separation from red-gap, meaning small dots in peripheral vision on a dense table can momentarily confuse. Not worth the risk for an analyst tool.

## 3. Typography — Inter + JetBrains Mono, self-hosted

**Switch from the system stack.** Segoe UI on Windows (which runs most Belgian consultancy desktops) lacks the refined tabular figures that Inter ships with, and screenshots will look inconsistent between Mac and Windows when shared in client deliverables. The ~220KB one-time font cost is trivial against the UX gain on 12–14px body text and 10–12px metadata.

### Recommended pairing: Inter + JetBrains Mono

- **Headings (32/24/18 px):** Inter 600/700, letter-spacing -0.01em at ≥24px
- **Body & UI (14 px):** Inter 400/500 with `font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums slashed-zero`
- **Metadata (12 px):** Inter 500 at neutral-500
- **Numeric / KPI big-numbers / IDs / axis labels:** JetBrains Mono 500
- **License:** Free, SIL OFL — no procurement overhead
- **Total weight:** ~220KB woff2 subset Latin-Extended, cached
- **Multilingual coverage:** both fonts ship full NL/FR diacritics (ë ï é è ê ç à ù)

```css
:root {
  --font-sans: 'Inter', ui-sans-serif, system-ui, -apple-system, 'Segoe UI', sans-serif;
  --font-mono: 'JetBrains Mono', ui-monospace, 'SF Mono', Menlo, monospace;
}
html { font-family: var(--font-sans); font-feature-settings: 'cv11','ss01','ss03'; }
table, .tabular, .kpi-number { font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums slashed-zero; }
code, pre, .mono { font-family: var(--font-mono); }
```

### Alternatives evaluated

- **Geist Sans + Geist Mono** — equally valid, free (OFL), slightly more geometric. Pick if the founder wants Vercel-adjacent precision over Inter's humanist warmth.
- **IBM Plex Sans + Plex Mono** — strongest "editorial/enterprise" flavor, slightly heavier (~250KB), free (OFL). Best paired with the graphite palette for a Bloomberg-lite feel.
- **Paid options (Söhne, Haffer, GT America, Aeonik, Untitled Sans):** all beautiful, all $300–3K+ for commercial web/desktop bundles depending on page views and seats. **Not worth the cost or procurement friction at hackathon/MVP stage.** Revisit at Series A.

## 4. Styleguide skeleton

Ready to lift into a project doc and expand. Written to be implementation-specific, not decorative.

### 4.1 Brand essence

**Tagline (working):** *See what your clients will need — before they do.* Seven words, benefit-first, matches the Linear/Notion pattern. Alternates: "Forecast the skills your market is buying" / "Yield management for consultancy staffing."

**Positioning one-liner:** *Skills demand forecasting for Belgian digital consultancies — predict the skill-mix your clients will buy in the next 3–6 months by fusing your CRM pipeline with external market signals.*

**Voice and tone:**
- **Confident but not cold.** Write like a senior analyst briefing a managing partner, not like marketing.
- **Specific over general.** "Java architects in Flanders up 23% in 30 days" beats "Trending skills nearby."
- **Cite the signal.** Every prediction shows its sources — the brand voice matches the product value.
- **No breathless AI.** Avoid "revolutionary," "AI-powered," "unlock," "supercharge." Use "predicted," "forecast," "signal," "source."

### 4.2 Logo principles (briefing, not design)

The logo should express **navigation, foresight, and precision** in that order. A wordmark with an optional geometric monogram mark — no mascots, no illustrative metaphors, no gradients. The mark should work at 16×16 favicon and on a 400×400 avatar. If Lodestr is chosen, an abstract star/compass bearing point in graphite (not indigo or blue, to avoid signal collision) is the natural direction. The monogram must read on both `#FFFFFF` and `#0E0D0A`. Avoid: ship wheels, crystal balls, eye-in-triangle, upward arrows with "growth" connotations.

### 4.3 Color system (usage rules)

Three color families, three rulebooks. Never mix them.

- **Brand family (graphite + cocoa):** chrome only — logo, primary nav, primary button, focus rings, link hover. Never inside a data cell or chart mark.
- **Semantic family (red/amber/emerald for status; blue/violet/cyan/emerald for signal source):** data only — inside cells, legends, heatmaps, chart marks, status pills. Never as chrome. Never as a body color.
- **Neutrals (stone scale 50–950):** everything else — surfaces, borders, body text, dividers, disabled states, low-emphasis UI.

**Dark mode:** canvas `#0E0D0A`, card `#1A1814`, body text neutral-200, subtle text neutral-400. Primary button stays brand-700 with white text only on the light theme; on dark, use neutral-100 ghost with brand-700 hover fill.

### 4.4 Typography scale

Locked to the UX spec's 32/24/18/14/12 ladder, Inter throughout:

| Role | Size | Weight | Tracking | Line height |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Display | 32px | 600 | -0.015em | 1.15 |
| H1 | 24px | 600 | -0.01em | 1.2 |
| H2 | 18px | 600 | -0.005em | 1.3 |
| Body | 14px | 400 | 0 | 1.5 |
| Metadata | 12px | 500 | +0.005em | 1.4 |
| KPI number (mono) | 24–32px | JetBrains Mono 500 | 0 | 1.1 |
| Table cell number (mono) | 13px | JetBrains Mono 400 | 0 | 1.3 |

Apply `tabular-nums slashed-zero` on every element containing numbers in a grid or KPI context.

### 4.5 Iconography direction

Stay on **lucide-react** (per UX spec). Rules:

- 16px in dense rows, 20px in nav, 24px in empty states. No custom icon sizes.
- **Stroke: 1.5px default, 1.75px at 16px to compensate for rendering.** Never use lucide's "fill" variants — they break visual rhythm.
- **Color:** neutral-500 for passive; neutral-700 on hover; signal colors only when the icon is conveying semantic state (e.g., AlertTriangle in red-gap on a critical row).
- Signal-source icons: procurement = `FileText` blue, trend = `TrendingUp` violet, posting = `Briefcase` cyan, pipeline = `GitBranch` emerald. Lock this mapping in a constants file.

### 4.6 Data visualization principles (the heart of the product)

**Rule 0:** the six semantic colors are the data palette. Do not introduce chart palettes from recharts, Tableau-10, or Observable-10. Every chart color must carry the same meaning across every chart.

- **Categorical by status:** red-gap, amber-watch, emerald-covered. Used in coverage bars, supply-vs-demand deltas, risk indicators.
- **Categorical by signal source:** procurement-blue, trend-violet, posting-cyan, pipeline-emerald. Used in stacked bars showing where a prediction comes from.
- **Sequential / heatmap (demand intensity):** use a single-hue ramp of the signal being measured. Example for demand heatmap: neutral-100 → amber-300 → amber-600 → red-700. Nine steps max. Generate with a deterministic function, not hand-picked.
- **Confidence indication:** never use color alone. Use a combination of (a) opacity 40%/70%/100% for low/medium/high confidence, (b) a small pill badge reading "62% conf.", and (c) border style dashed/dotted/solid. This triple-coding is ADA-critical and matches what Bloomberg Terminal and Observable users expect.
- **Grid lines:** neutral-200 at 1px, never neutral-300 or darker. Axis labels neutral-500, 11px, mono.
- **Empty state in chart:** "Not enough signal yet" in neutral-500, never a fabricated placeholder curve.

### 4.7 Motion and micro-interactions

Analyst-respecting means **fast, subtle, and never decorative.**

- **Hover:** 100ms ease-out, brand color shift only, no scale, no shadow pop.
- **Panel/modal:** 180ms ease-out, 4px Y-translate + opacity. No bounces, no elastic.
- **Data update:** when a cell value changes, a 600ms fade-from-amber-tint back to default. Never a flashing animation.
- **Route change:** instant, zero transition. Analysts context-switch often; motion would enrage Sebastiaan.
- **Reduced motion:** respect `prefers-reduced-motion`; disable all but opacity fades.
- **Never:** confetti, progress bar bounces, "loading" Lottie, tooltip wobble.

### 4.8 Writing guidelines

**Use:** *forecast* (the product's core verb — use over "predict" which reads slightly more fortune-teller), *signal, gap, coverage, pipeline, skill-mix, demand, supply, bench, utilization, yield, watchlist, portfolio.*

**Avoid:** *predict* (as a marketing verb — fine inside "prediction" as a noun for a specific output), *AI-powered, unlock, supercharge, revolutionary, game-changing, actionable insights, data-driven* (overused — assume the reader knows it is).

**Gap vs shortfall:** use **gap** — shorter, reads on a dashboard label, is the same word in Dutch and decoded by French speakers.

**Numbers:** always include the unit and the timeframe. "+23 roles, 30-day rolling" not "+23 trending."

**Multilingual:** EN is the default product language. When localizing:
- NL: *voorspelling* (forecast), *hiaat / tekort* (gap — **hiaat** is closer in register), *signaal, pijplijn.*
- FR: *prévision* (forecast), *écart* (gap — never "manque" which reads as deficiency), *signal, pipeline.*
- Avoid machine-translating product labels in a rush — "gap" has three FR translations with different business connotations. Get a bilingual reviewer per release.

## 5. Competitive and inspiration synthesis

The scan across ~20 peer products surfaces a clear pattern and a clear gap. **The pattern:** premium 2025 B2B analytics brands are converging on monochrome-plus-one-accent with a humanist sans-serif, and differentiate almost entirely through the *accent hue choice* and the *wordmark voice*. **The gap:** no one in the European talent-intelligence category is executing this pattern well — the competitors are mostly stuck in 2018-era SaaS-blue-plus-illustration.

### What the premium reference set is doing

Linear (indigo `#5E6AD2` + graphite, Haffer), Vercel (pure black + white, Geist), Notion (pure monochrome + in-doc color), Observable (near-black + curated data palette), Hex (near-black + magenta-violet accent), Causal (black + bright chart accents), Retool (warm olive neutral `#E9EBDF` + graphite — the true outlier), dbt (orange `#FF694A` — the other outlier). **The shared move is letting data carry color and keeping chrome quiet.** Your UX spec already commits to this by locking six semantic colors — you just need to honor it with a brand that doesn't fight.

### Predictive vs descriptive analytics — the tonal split

Predictive tools (Pigment, Runway, Causal, Anaplan) lean **confident and future-facing** in copy ("Prepare for Anything" at Pigment, "confident decisions" at Runway, explicit agentic-AI messaging across all). Descriptive tools (Metabase, Looker, Mode, Tableau, Superset) lean **neutral and utilitarian**. Visually the predictive set is slightly more willing to experiment — Pigment uses a red-yellow-blue tricolor and pairs DM Sans with Crimson Pro serif, which is a vastly warmer choice than Looker's Google-blue sobriety. **This is permission to be slightly warm, slightly editorial, slightly distinctive** — which the graphite + cocoa palette directly operationalizes.

### European B2B house style

Qonto moved from Electric Purple `#7a69e6` to black + white + mint/peach/mustard secondaries and PolySans type — a warmer, more editorial rebrand. Pigment is explicit about "French creative heritage" as a brand asset. Doctolib had to *darken* its blue to hit WCAG AA. Pennylane and PayFit are more conservative. The common thread is **warmer neutrals than American equivalents, more typographic confidence, less illustration-as-brand.** For a Belgian product this is the home court — a warm stone neutral scale and a confident wordmark will read as "European B2B in 2026" rather than transplanted Silicon Valley.

### Talent-intelligence competitors — the differentiation opportunity

The direct competitor set (TechWolf, Lightcast, Beamery, Eightfold, Gloat, 365Talents, Fuel50) is dominated by blue-and-purple gradients, stock-photography hero sections, and Workday-adjacent enterprise aesthetics. **None of them look like a 2026 analyst tool.** Entering this market with a Linear-polished, Bloomberg-serious, editorially warm brand is a genuine category positioning — not just a visual preference. It's what Sebastiaan (and every power-user managing partner like him) actually wants on a screen for eight hours.

### Eight cues to apply

- Wordmark-led logo with optional geometric monogram; no illustrative mark.
- Tagline 6–8 words, benefit-first, outcome-specific (the Pigment/Linear formula).
- One accent color only — cocoa — used sparingly enough that it stays a signature.
- Warm-tinted stone neutrals, not cool slate, to signal European-editorial not American-clinical.
- Mono typography used on all numbers, always, to set the analyst tone immediately.
- Dark mode as a first-class theme, not an afterthought — analysts on long sessions default to it.
- Every prediction shows its signal source — brand voice and product value are the same thing.
- Never use stock photography; use the dashboard itself as hero imagery (Retool and Linear's pattern).

### Three anti-patterns visible in competitors — do not replicate

- Hero sections with smiling-diverse-people stock photos overlaid on gradient blurs (Beamery, Eightfold).
- Mid-blue-plus-purple gradient brand palettes that collide with semantic UI (Lightcast, Fuel50).
- "AI-powered talent intelligence" without a single numeric example on the homepage (most of the category).

## Conclusion — what changes versus what stays

Your UX spec made the single most important brand decision already: committing the color system to semantics. Everything in this report follows from respecting that commitment. The brand should be **named like a navigation instrument, colored like a well-lit newsroom, and typeset like a terminal.** That is what separates "Bloomberg energy but approachable" from yet another SaaS-blue dashboard, and it is what Sebastiaan's eyes will thank you for at 7pm on a Thursday.

The non-obvious insight from the peer scan: **the entire data-analytics B2B category is already in the monochrome-plus-accent paradigm, but the talent-intelligence sub-category has not caught up.** Entering that gap with a disciplined, warm, editorial brand is a genuine positioning advantage — not decoration, and not a hackathon indulgence. The specific choices (Lodestr, graphite `#1A1D24` + cocoa `#6B4E3D`, Inter + JetBrains Mono) are the concrete expression of that positioning, but the underlying move is more important than any single pick: **let the data be the brand, and make the chrome disappear.**

Formal trademark clearance on the top three names and a WHOIS sweep on `.ai`/`.com` are the two hard blockers before any of this ships publicly — both are a half-day of work and should be done before the next design sprint.
